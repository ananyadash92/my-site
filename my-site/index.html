About

I became the bridge between the boardroom and the shop floor.

I'm Ananya Dash. Fifteen years inside Tata Steel — across supply chain, customer service, HR, marketing & sales, and enterprise IT — taught me one lesson: transformation fails when leaders can't translate complexity into action. I became the bridge between what leadership wanted and what teams understood. Between data and decision.

At Tata Steel, I led CX transformation within the One IT ecosystem, converting raw customer interactions into governance-ready executive narratives. But the pattern I kept seeing wasn't just organisational — it was personal. Sharp, capable mid-senior leaders were stalling. Not because they lacked skill, but because they lacked clarity. That's when coaching became the second track of my practice, built on ICF methodology and years of doing the work informally.

Today, I work independently — bringing that enterprise-tested thinking to organisations navigating transformation and leaders navigating what comes next. Coaching and transformation are two expressions of the same thing: moving through complexity with clarity.

What I Believe

  • CX is not about happiness. It's about reliability and trust.
  • Visuals must earn their place. So must every sentence in a strategy deck.
  • Governance-friendly storytelling beats raw data every time.
  • Action orientation over diagnosis looping — always.
  • If there's no "so what," the analysis isn't finished.
