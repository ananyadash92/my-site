## ABOUT

### The Short Version

I'm Ananya Dash. I've spent 15 years inside Tata Steel — not in one lane, but across supply chain, customer service, HR, marketing & sales, and enterprise IT. Every role taught me the same lesson: transformation fails when leaders can't translate complexity into action.

### The Longer Version

Early in my career, I watched brilliant strategies die in execution — not because the thinking was wrong, but because nobody bridged the gap between the boardroom and the shop floor. Between what leadership wanted and what teams understood. Between data and decision.

I became that bridge.

At Tata Steel, I led CX transformation within the One IT ecosystem — converting raw customer and sales interaction data into governance-ready executive narratives. I built frameworks that turned ambiguity into structure, frontline noise into leadership signal.

But the pattern I kept seeing wasn't just organisational. It was personal. Mid-senior leaders — sharp, experienced, capable — were stalling. Not because they lacked skill, but because they lacked clarity. They were navigating AI disruption, org restructuring, and career pivots without a thinking partner who understood both the system and the self.

That's when coaching stopped being a side interest and became the second track of my practice. I trained with ICF methodology — not to add a credential, but because I'd already been doing the work informally for years. Coaching and transformation are two expressions of the same thing: helping people and organisations move through complexity with clarity.

### What I Believe

- CX is not about happiness. It's about reliability and trust.
- Visuals must earn their place. So must every sentence in a strategy deck.
- Governance-friendly storytelling beats raw data every time.
- Action orientation over diagnosis looping — always.
- If there's no "so what," the analysis isn't finished.
