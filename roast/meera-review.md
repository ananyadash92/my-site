# Meera Nambiar — Target Customer Review

**Context: I'm a VP of Customer Experience at a mid-size Indian manufacturing firm. We're 18 months into a digital transformation. My CX data is a mess. My leadership team keeps asking me for "insights" but what they really want is a narrative they can present to the board. I found this site through a LinkedIn post.**

**Rating: 7/10 — She gets my problem. I'm not yet sure she can solve it.**

---

## My First Reaction

The headline hit me: "Your transformation has data. It needs a narrative." YES. That's exactly my problem. I have 14 dashboards and nobody reads any of them. I got excited.

The subhead was fine. The stat bar — 15 years, 5 functional domains — tells me she's been inside a system like mine. Not an outsider. That matters.

## Where I Got Hooked

The **Before/After table** in the Transformation & CX Consulting card. Specifically this row:

> Before: "Insight reports that describe problems"
> After: "Reports that prescribe next steps with clear accountability"

That's my team's exact failure mode. We describe beautifully. We prescribe nothing. She clearly knows this world.

**"Cross-functional orchestration — I speak every department's language"** — this is the thing that would make me pick up the phone. My biggest pain isn't the data. It's that sales, CS, and IT all describe the same customer differently. If she can actually bridge that, she's worth whatever she charges.

## Where I Got Lost

1. **I don't know what a "Clarity Call" actually is.** Is it a sales call? A diagnostic? Do I get something out of it? The CTA section says "30 minutes of structured thinking" — okay, but what's the output? Do I get a written summary? A framework? A proposal? I'm a VP — I need to justify 30 minutes on my calendar. "Structured thinking" isn't enough.

2. **I can't tell if she works independently or through Tata Steel.** The About section says she's spent 15 years "inside Tata Steel." Is she still there? Is this a side practice? Is she full-time independent? This matters enormously for an enterprise engagement. If I hire her for a 4-8 week scoped brief, is she available? Does she have a team? Am I getting HER or someone she's trained?

3. **The coaching section confused my visit.** I came here for CX consulting. But half the site is about leadership coaching. The two-track structure is intellectually clean, but as a visitor I had to scroll past a full coaching pitch to get to the CTA. Consider: if I'm an enterprise buyer, the coaching card is noise. If I'm a coaching buyer, the consulting card is noise. **The dual positioning, while strategically sound, creates friction for each specific buyer.**

4. **The testimonials didn't help.** I could tell they weren't real. "Senior Stakeholder, Enterprise Transformation" isn't a person. This actually made me more cautious, not less. If she's as good as the copy suggests, where are the real endorsements? Even one LinkedIn recommendation screenshot would be more convincing.

5. **No case studies.** The Before/After tables hint at what she does, but I want to see a real example. "Here's what a customer visit report looked like before. Here's what it looked like after." One concrete artifact would convert me faster than five paragraphs of copy.

## Questions I Still Have

- Is she currently at Tata Steel or independent?
- What does a 4-8 week engagement actually look like? What are the deliverables?
- Has she worked with companies outside Tata Steel?
- What does it cost? (Even a range would help me decide if I'm in the right ballpark)
- Can she work with my team directly, or is it advisory only?

## Would I Convert?

**Probably, but not from this visit.** I'd bookmark the site, look her up on LinkedIn, and wait for another signal (a post, a referral, a conference appearance) before reaching out. The site builds credibility but doesn't close. It's missing the "last mile" — a concrete next step that feels low-risk.

If the "Book a Clarity Call" button went to a Calendly with clear time slots and a 2-line description of what happens in the call, I'd book it today.

## Net-Net

She understands my world better than 90% of consultants I've evaluated. The copy is specific and credible. But the site asks me to commit before giving me enough to decide. Add one case study, clarify the engagement model, and give me a real booking link — and I'm in.
