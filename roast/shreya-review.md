# Shreya Iyer — Brand Strategist Review

**Rating: 7.5/10 — Strong foundation, some identity leaks**

---

## What's Working

1. **The headline is a keeper.** "Your transformation has data. It needs a narrative." — this is genuine positioning, not a tagline committee output. It's specific to what Ananya actually does. I'd remember it.

2. **The "Two Tracks, One Throughline" framing is smart.** It immediately tells me this isn't a scattered generalist. The consulting/coaching duality is the brand, and it's articulated clearly.

3. **The Before/After tables are a power move.** They demonstrate the thinking style — structured, comparative, action-oriented — before you even hire her. The medium IS the message.

4. **"Net-Net" as a CTA section header is on-brand.** It's insider language that signals "I'm one of you" to enterprise leaders. Not everyone will get it, but the right people will.

5. **The beliefs section is genuine.** "CX is not about happiness. It's about reliability and trust." — that's a real point of view. It's not trying to please everyone.

## What's NOT Working

1. **The About section is too long.** Five paragraphs is an essay. This is a website, not a keynote. The first two paragraphs are strong — the "bridge" metaphor lands. But by paragraph four, I'm skimming. The coaching origin story ("That's when coaching became the second track...") is buried where nobody will read it. **This is your most important differentiator and it's in the least-read position.**

2. **"Transformation Consulting & Leadership Coaching" as the eyebrow is generic.** It reads like a LinkedIn headline, not a brand position. Every second consultant on LinkedIn says some version of this. The headline below it is SO much better — lead with that energy everywhere.

3. **The testimonials are obviously fake.** I understand they're placeholders, but shipping with placeholder testimonials is worse than shipping with no testimonials. A visitor will clock these as fabricated in under 3 seconds. The "Senior Stakeholder, Enterprise Transformation" attribution is the tell — no real human has that title. **This actively damages credibility.**

4. **No photo of Ananya anywhere.** This is a personal brand site for coaching and consulting. People hire people. The absence of a face makes this feel like a template, not a person. The copy says "I became that bridge" but the design says "I could be anyone."

5. **The voice drops in the contact section.** "Start a Conversation" and "Whether you're leading a transformation..." is competent but generic. Compare it to "Let's Get to the Net-Net" — that section has personality. The contact section sounds like it was written by a different person.

6. **"Clarity Call" is good but unexplained.** What happens in 30 minutes? What do I walk away with? The CTA section says "structured thinking" but doesn't give me a concrete deliverable. Enterprise buyers need to know what they're getting before they book time.

## Net-Net

The brand voice is 80% there — distinctive when it's at its best ("Not decoration — evidence"), generic when it slips ("Start a Conversation"). The structure and thinking are clearly above-average. But the site needs a face, needs the testimonials resolved, and needs the About section edited to half its length. The strongest writing on this site is in the services section. The weakest is in the transitions between sections.
