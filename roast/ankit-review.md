# Ankit Verma — Growth Marketer Review

**Rating: 6.5/10 — Beautiful site, weak conversion architecture**

---

## 5-Second Test

I loaded the page and within 5 seconds I got: "Something about transformation and data narratives. She's a consultant of some kind." The headline is strong but the subhead dilutes it. "For organisations navigating AI transformation, AND for leaders navigating what comes next" — that's two audiences in one sentence. My brain can't decide which one I am.

**Verdict:** Value prop lands at ~70%. I know WHAT she does, but I'm not sure WHO she does it for — me specifically.

## Conversion Path Analysis

### Hero → CTA Flow
- **Primary CTA "Book a Clarity Call"** appears in: hero, nav, CTA section, and contact section. That's good frequency.
- **BUT: Every single CTA points to #contact** (a form). There's no external booking link. No Calendly. No calendar picker. The call-to-action says "Book" but the destination says "Fill out a form and wait." That's a conversion killer. "Book" implies immediacy. A form implies friction.

### Drop-Off Points (predicted)

1. **After Hero → About transition:** The hero is punchy and fast. The About section opens with a long narrative. The energy cliff is steep. Visitors who were hooked by the headline will bounce when they hit a 5-paragraph essay about someone they don't know yet.

2. **Mid-Services section:** The service cards are information-dense. An enterprise buyer scanning on mobile will see: paragraph + table + bullet list + engagement types — and that's just ONE card. They'll scroll past without reading the table (which is the best part). The information hierarchy within each card doesn't guide the eye to the most compelling content first.

3. **Testimonials → CTA transition:** Fake testimonials create a trust dip right before the conversion ask. The reader's brain registers "these aren't real" (even subconsciously), and then the very next section asks them to commit their time. Bad sequencing.

## What's Missing

1. **No social proof above the fold.** The stat bar (15+ years, 5 domains, ICF) is good but it's below the fold on most screens. A "Tata Steel" logo or "As seen in" bar near the hero would establish credibility instantly.

2. **No lead magnet or low-commitment entry point.** The only conversion options are "Book a Call" (high commitment) and "Fill out a form" (medium commitment). Where's the email capture? A downloadable framework? A "Get my 5-question transformation audit" PDF? Enterprise buyers don't book calls from a website on first visit. They need a nurture step.

3. **No pricing signals whatsoever.** Not even a range. Not even "starting from." Enterprise buyers expect at minimum an indication of engagement model. "6-session structured coaching arc" — what does that cost? Is it 50K INR or 5L? The coaching track especially needs a signal, because coaches range from 5K/session to 5L/session.

4. **No "Who This Is NOT For" section.** Ananya's positioning is specific. Saying who you don't serve is as powerful as saying who you do. It would sharpen the conversion for the right visitors and save time filtering the wrong ones.

5. **Meta tags are minimal.** No Open Graph tags, no Twitter card, no structured data. If someone shares this on LinkedIn (her primary channel), it'll show a generic preview with no image. For a personal brand site, the LinkedIn share preview IS the first impression for most visitors.

## What I'd A/B Test

- Hero subhead: single-audience version ("For enterprise leaders navigating AI transformation") vs current dual-audience version
- CTA button: "Book a Clarity Call" vs "Get Your Free Transformation Audit"
- Adding a Tata Steel logo/mention near the hero
- Testimonials section: remove entirely vs keep with real quotes

## Net-Net

This site would impress someone who lands on it — the design is premium and the copy is sharp. But it's built as a brochure, not a conversion engine. There's no nurture path, no low-friction entry point, no booking tool, and no pricing signal. A mid-senior enterprise leader will visit, think "impressive," and leave without converting. The site needs to answer: "What's my next step if I'm interested but not ready to call?"
