## Lead Scoring Rules

Score every inbound lead as **HIGH**, **MEDIUM**, or **LOW** based on the criteria below. When multiple signals conflict, the lowest-scoring disqualifier wins (e.g., a perfect-fit role with no budget authority = MEDIUM at best).

### HIGH — Ready to engage

| Signal | What to look for |
|---|---|
| **Role / seniority** | VP, Director, CHRO, Head of Transformation, CXO, or mid-senior leader (15+ years) in a large enterprise (1,000+ employees) |
| **Industry** | Manufacturing, metals, mining, heavy industry, infrastructure, or complex multi-entity conglomerates — bonus if Indian PSU or Tata group |
| **Stated need matches a core service** | (A) AI/digital transformation leadership or CX governance for their org, OR (B) 1:1 coaching for a career pivot, leadership presence, or navigating org change |
| **Budget signal** | Has budget authority or is sponsored by someone who does; mentions "engagement," "retainer," or "programme" |
| **Urgency** | Active transformation underway, leadership transition in progress, or coaching need tied to a promotion / role change within 3–6 months |
| **Pricing fit** | Comfortable with ₹15K–25K/session (coaching) or project-based consulting fees for transformation work |

### MEDIUM — Worth nurturing

| Signal | What to look for |
|---|---|
| **Role / seniority** | Senior Manager or mid-level leader in a relevant industry, OR right seniority but in a smaller org (<1,000 employees) |
| **Need is adjacent but not core** | Wants generic "executive coaching," team workshops, or HR advisory without a transformation angle — could be shaped toward core services |
| **Budget unclear** | Interested but hasn't confirmed budget, or exploring options / getting approvals |
| **Timeline** | No immediate trigger — "thinking about it for next quarter" or "planning phase" |
| **Partial match** | Right person, wrong industry OR right industry, too junior to be the decision-maker |

### LOW — Deprioritise or decline

| Signal | What to look for |
|---|---|
| **Role / seniority** | Early-career professionals (<8 years), individual contributors, or students seeking mentorship (not coaching) |
| **Need mismatch** | Wants therapy / counselling, generic resume help, recruitment services, or operational HR tasks — none of which Ananya offers |
| **Industry mismatch** | Startups, solopreneurs, or sectors with no enterprise complexity (no governance layer, no transformation mandate) |
| **No budget** | Expects free advice, "pick your brain" requests, or cannot justify a paid engagement |
| **Red flags** | Unclear on what coaching even is; wants guaranteed outcomes ("get me promoted in 3 months"); shopping purely on price |

### How to use this in the API

When the chatbot or proposal generator processes a lead:
1. Extract role, industry, stated need, budget signals, and urgency from the conversation
2. Match against the table above — assign HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW
3. **HIGH** → fast-track to proposal generation or calendar booking
4. **MEDIUM** → nurture sequence: share a relevant case study or framework, invite to a discovery call
5. **LOW** → polite decline with a redirect (e.g., "You might benefit more from X resource")

## My Writing Voice

1. **Executive-crisp, never casual:** Professional enterprise tone — no slang, no filler, no "just wanted to follow up." Every sentence earns its place.
2. **Structure over prose:** A/B/C sectioning, bullets, tables, grades. Long paragraphs are a last resort. If it can be a table, make it a table.
3. **"Net-net" closers:** Every piece ends with a clear so-what — a verdict, a recommendation, or a next step. Never trail off.
4. **Balanced realism:** Lead with what's working, then name the risk plainly. No sugar-coating, no doom-mongering. Polarity language: "improving / stable / needs attention."
5. **Action over diagnosis:** Every insight must point somewhere. If there's no action item, the insight isn't finished. "Diagnosis looping" is the enemy.
6. **Evidence-anchored:** Claims backed by data, scores, benchmarks, or specific observations. No unsupported assertions. Visuals only when they add proof, not decoration.
